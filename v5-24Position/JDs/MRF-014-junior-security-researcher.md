## 1. Role Purpose

The Junior Security Researcher strengthens the Cybersecurity BU's offensive security (OffSec) capability by supporting hands-on vulnerability research, malware analysis, and tooling. Working closely with senior researchers, the role helps surface, understand, and document emerging threats that feed into our offensive security and threat intelligence offerings. It is a foundational, growth-oriented position designed to develop deep technical research skills while delivering tangible value to client engagements and internal capability.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Assist senior researchers in identifying, analysing, and validating software vulnerabilities across target systems and applications.
- Analyse malware samples and security threats to extract behaviours, indicators of compromise, and actionable findings.
- Document research findings clearly and accurately in structured reports, advisories, and internal knowledge bases.
- Develop and maintain research tools, scripts, and automation that improve the speed and repeatability of the team's work.
- Participate in Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions to sharpen offensive skills and represent the BU's research capability.
- Reproduce and triage reported vulnerabilities, supporting verification and proof-of-concept development under senior guidance.
- Track emerging threats, exploits, and disclosures, summarising relevant developments for the wider OffSec team.
- Collaborate with offensive security and threat intelligence colleagues to translate research into engagement-ready insights.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Cybersecurity, or a related field.
- 2+ years of experience in security research or a closely related technical security discipline.
- Basic working knowledge of vulnerability research concepts and methodologies.
- Practical programming skills in Python and C/C++.
- Capture The Flag (CTF) experience preferred.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Experience with malware analysis.
- Knowledge of reverse engineering.
- Participation in security conferences or community events.
- Published security research or technical write-ups.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Programming and scripting in Python and C/C++ for tooling, automation, and proof-of-concept work.
- Familiarity with vulnerability research techniques (fuzzing, code review, debugging).
- Exposure to malware analysis (static and dynamic) and reverse engineering tools such as Ghidra, IDA, x64dbg, or radare2.
- Comfort working in Linux and Windows environments.
- Use of debuggers, disassemblers, and common offensive security tooling.
- Sound understanding of operating system internals, memory, and common vulnerability classes.
- Version control (Git) and clear technical documentation practices.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- Curiosity and a strong drive to learn, experiment, and dig deep into how systems work.
- Analytical thinking and methodical problem-solving when investigating complex technical issues.
- Clear written and verbal communication, especially in documenting and explaining findings.
- Ownership and reliability in following through on assigned research tasks.
- Collaboration and openness to feedback and mentorship from senior researchers.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**

- None mandatory.

**Preferred**

- OSCP (Offensive Security Certified Professional)
- GREM (GIAC Reverse Engineering Malware) or equivalent malware analysis / reverse engineering certification

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Volume and quality of research contributions delivered in support of senior researchers within the first 6–12 months.
- Number of vulnerabilities or malware behaviours successfully analysed, validated, and documented.
- Quality, clarity, and timeliness of research reports and advisories produced.
- Research tools or automation built or improved that enhance team efficiency.
- Active participation in CTF competitions and measurable growth in technical capability.
- Demonstrated progression in independence and ownership of assigned research tasks.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Lead Security Researcher and works as part of the OffSec team within the Cybersecurity BU, collaborating closely with senior researchers and partnering with threat intelligence and offensive security colleagues to turn findings into engagement-ready insights.
