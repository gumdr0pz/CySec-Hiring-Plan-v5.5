## 1. Role Purpose

The Senior Sales Specialist (Enterprise) drives top-line revenue growth for the Cybersecurity BU by winning and expanding strategic enterprise accounts across Malaysia and the wider region. The role converts the BU's offensive security, threat intelligence, DevSecOps, blockchain security, and consulting capabilities into signed engagements, directly fuelling the recurring-revenue trajectory that underpins continued team expansion. It is the commercial bridge between client business needs and the BU's technical delivery teams.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Build and deepen long-term relationships with enterprise clients, positioning the BU as a trusted cybersecurity partner across multiple buying units and decision-makers.
- Identify, qualify, and pursue new business opportunities within the enterprise segment, expanding the pipeline of high-value cybersecurity and professional-services engagements.
- Develop compelling proposals and lead participation in competitive bids, RFPs, and tenders to maximise win rates and deal value.
- Own and achieve quarterly and annual sales targets, accurately forecasting revenue and maintaining a healthy, well-managed pipeline.
- Collaborate closely with technical and delivery teams to scope solutions, shape Statements of Work, and ensure proposed engagements are deliverable and commercially sound.
- Translate the BU's offensive security, threat intelligence, DevSecOps, and blockchain security offerings into clear client value propositions tailored to each account.
- Negotiate commercial terms, pricing, and contracts to close deals that balance client value with BU profitability.
- Maintain disciplined CRM hygiene and provide regular, data-driven reporting on pipeline, forecast, and account health to the BU Head.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Business, Marketing, or a related field.
- 5+ years of experience in enterprise sales.
- Demonstrated experience in cybersecurity or technology sales.
- Strong, active network within the enterprise sector.
- Excellent negotiation skills with a track record of closing complex deals.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Master's degree in Business Administration (MBA).
- Experience with complex solution selling and multi-stakeholder sales cycles.
- Working knowledge of the cybersecurity market and its key players.
- Previous experience in a startup or high-growth environment.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Consultative and solution-selling methodologies applied to technology and security services.
- Proposal, bid, and RFP/tender management end to end.
- Pipeline management, forecasting, and CRM tooling (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot, or similar).
- Commercial acumen: pricing, margin awareness, and contract/SOW structuring.
- Working familiarity with cybersecurity domains — offensive security, threat intelligence, DevSecOps, and blockchain security — sufficient to converse credibly with technical buyers.
- Account planning and stakeholder mapping for enterprise organisations.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- Results-driven ownership, with personal accountability for targets and outcomes.
- Strong communication and executive-level presence in client and internal settings.
- Cross-functional collaboration, working effectively with technical and delivery teams.
- Analytical thinking to qualify opportunities and prioritise high-value pursuits.
- Resilience and persistence through long, competitive enterprise sales cycles.
- Customer-centric mindset that builds trust and long-term partnerships.

## 7. Certifications

**Required:**
- None mandatory.

**Preferred:**
- Recognised sales methodology certifications (e.g., MEDDIC, SPIN, Miller Heiman, Challenger).
- Foundational cybersecurity awareness credentials (e.g., CompTIA Security+ or vendor security sales accreditations).

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Achievement of assigned quarterly and annual signed-revenue / SOW targets.
- Volume and value of qualified pipeline generated within the first 6–12 months.
- Win rate on submitted proposals, bids, and tenders.
- Number of new enterprise logos acquired and existing accounts expanded.
- Forecast accuracy and CRM data quality maintained throughout the period.
- Contribution to the BU's recurring-revenue growth trajectory.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports on a dotted line to the BU Head and works in close partnership with the Cybersecurity BU's technical and delivery teams to scope, propose, and close enterprise engagements. As a senior individual contributor, the specialist is expected to share best practice and provide commercial guidance to less experienced sales colleagues.

## 10. Compensation & Benefits

- **Monthly Base Salary:** RM4,900 – RM7,840
- **Annual Base Salary:** RM58,800 – RM94,080

In addition to base salary, the BU offers statutory contributions (EPF/SOCSO/EIS), medical coverage, training and certification sponsorship, and a performance-based bonus. Sales-related variable incentives may apply in line with company policy.

## 11. Career Progression

The natural next step is progression to a Sales Lead / Sales Manager or Key Account Director role, taking on team leadership and ownership of a broader enterprise portfolio within the Cybersecurity BU.
