## 1. Role Purpose
The Senior Blockchain Developer builds and secures the decentralized applications, smart contracts, and protocol-level solutions that underpin the Cybersecurity BU's blockchain practice. The role exists to deliver production-grade, audit-ready blockchain systems for our clients while strengthening the BU's reputation for secure-by-design distributed ledger engineering. By combining deep development expertise with a security-first mindset, this position directly supports the growth of our blockchain security and consulting revenue streams.

## 2. Key Responsibilities
- Develop robust blockchain applications and smart contracts (primarily on Ethereum/Solidity) that meet client requirements for functionality, gas efficiency, and security.
- Conduct smart contract audits and code reviews to identify vulnerabilities, recommend remediations, and produce clear, client-ready findings.
- Design decentralized architectures and protocol components, ensuring scalability, interoperability, and resilience against adversarial conditions.
- Apply cryptographic principles and secure coding practices throughout the development lifecycle to minimize attack surface.
- Mentor junior developers through code review, pair programming, and technical guidance, raising the overall capability of the Blockchain team.
- Contribute to blockchain research and internal tooling, evaluating emerging platforms, standards, and threat patterns to keep the BU at the leading edge.
- Collaborate with project and consulting teams to translate client objectives into well-scoped, deliverable engineering work.
- Document architectures, contracts, and audit outcomes to a professional standard suitable for client and regulatory review.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience
- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field.
- 5+ years of hands-on experience in blockchain development.
- Demonstrated experience with Ethereum, Solidity, and smart contract development.
- Working knowledge of cryptographic principles and their application to distributed systems.
- Strong programming skills across Solidity, Rust, and Go.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications
- Master's degree in a relevant field.
- Experience with other blockchain platforms such as Hyperledger or Solana.
- Hands-on smart contract security auditing experience.
- DeFi protocol development experience.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies
- Smart contract development and testing on EVM-based chains (Solidity, Hardhat, Foundry, Truffle).
- Proficiency in Rust and Go for protocol and backend blockchain components.
- Decentralized architecture and design patterns (tokens, oracles, upgradeable contracts, multi-sig).
- Smart contract security auditing, static/dynamic analysis, and gas optimization.
- Applied cryptography: hashing, digital signatures, key management, and consensus fundamentals.
- Familiarity with Web3 libraries, RPC tooling, and integration with decentralized storage and front-ends.
- Version control, CI/CD, and secure software development lifecycle practices.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies
- Ownership: takes end-to-end accountability for the quality and security of delivered code.
- Analytical thinking: rigorously evaluates designs and contracts for vulnerabilities and edge cases.
- Communication: explains complex blockchain concepts clearly to technical and non-technical stakeholders.
- Collaboration: works effectively across engineering, audit, and consulting teams to deliver client outcomes.
- Mentorship: invests in growing junior developers and sharing knowledge across the team.
- Continuous learning: stays current with a fast-moving blockchain and threat landscape.

## 7. Certifications
**Required**
- None mandatory.

**Preferred**
- Certified Blockchain Security Professional (CBSP) or equivalent.
- Smart contract auditing or blockchain security credentials.
- Relevant platform certifications (e.g., Ethereum, Hyperledger, Solana).

## 8. Key Performance Indicators
- Deliver smart contracts and applications that pass internal and external security review with minimal critical findings within the first 6-12 months.
- Complete assigned smart contract audits on schedule with clear, actionable remediation reports.
- Achieve high code quality and test coverage benchmarks across delivered work.
- Contribute to at least one reusable architecture pattern, tool, or research output for the Blockchain team.
- Demonstrably mentor junior developers, evidenced by improved review outcomes and reduced rework.
- Support on-time delivery of client engagements contributing to the BU's signed SOW pipeline.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context
This role reports to the Principal Blockchain Architect and works closely with fellow blockchain engineers, security auditors, and the BU's project and consulting teams. As a senior contributor, the Senior Blockchain Developer also mentors junior developers and provides technical leadership on engineering and audit deliverables.
