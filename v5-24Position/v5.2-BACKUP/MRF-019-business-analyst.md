## 1. Role Purpose

The Business Analyst sits within the PM Team of the Cybersecurity BU and exists to translate business needs into clear, actionable requirements that drive successful delivery across our offensive security, threat intelligence, DevSecOps, blockchain security, and consulting engagements. By eliciting, documenting, and structuring requirements, the role keeps client and internal stakeholders aligned with the technical teams delivering each solution. This reduces rework, sharpens project scope, and improves the predictability and quality of BU delivery as recurring revenue scales.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- **Elicit and document** business and functional requirements through interviews, workshops, and analysis, producing clear and traceable requirement artefacts.
- **Facilitate stakeholder workshops** with clients, delivery leads, and technical specialists to surface needs, resolve ambiguity, and build consensus on scope.
- **Author user stories and acceptance criteria** that are precise, testable, and ready for Agile delivery teams to estimate and execute.
- **Support project planning and prioritisation** by helping the Senior Project Manager translate requirements into backlogs, scope baselines, and delivery roadmaps.
- **Bridge business and technical perspectives**, ensuring proposed cybersecurity solutions align with stakeholder objectives, constraints, and compliance expectations.
- **Maintain requirements traceability** from inception through delivery, tracking changes and assessing their impact on scope, timeline, and effort.
- **Model and map business processes** to identify gaps, inefficiencies, and improvement opportunities relevant to engagement outcomes.
- **Validate delivered outcomes** against agreed acceptance criteria, supporting user acceptance and sign-off activities with stakeholders.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Business, Computer Science, or a related field.
- Minimum 2 years of experience in a business analysis role.
- Strong analytical and documentation skills, with the ability to structure complex information clearly.
- Hands-on experience working within Agile delivery methodologies.
- Demonstrated ability to communicate effectively with both business and technical stakeholders.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Experience supporting cybersecurity or broader technology projects.
- Familiarity with requirements management tools.
- Process modelling skills (e.g., BPMN, workflow mapping).
- Proven stakeholder facilitation and workshop experience.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Requirements elicitation, analysis, and documentation techniques.
- User story writing and acceptance criteria definition.
- Agile and Scrum delivery practices (backlog management, sprint planning support).
- Process modelling and business process mapping (e.g., BPMN, flow diagrams).
- Requirements management and collaboration tools (e.g., Jira, Confluence, Azure DevOps).
- Data analysis and clear reporting using productivity and visualisation tools.
- Working awareness of cybersecurity concepts and delivery contexts (advantageous).

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- **Analytical thinking** — breaks down ambiguous problems into structured, well-defined requirements.
- **Communication** — conveys ideas clearly in writing and conversation, adapting to business and technical audiences.
- **Collaboration** — works effectively across PM, delivery, and client teams to build shared understanding.
- **Ownership** — takes responsibility for the quality and completeness of requirements artefacts end to end.
- **Attention to detail** — ensures accuracy, consistency, and traceability across documentation.
- **Stakeholder management** — facilitates alignment and manages expectations across diverse participants.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**

- None mandatory.

**Preferred**

- BABOK / CBAP (or equivalent business analysis certification).
- Agile / Scrum certification (e.g., CSM, PSM, PSPO).

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Requirements artefacts (user stories, acceptance criteria) accepted by delivery teams with minimal rework within the first 6 months.
- Reduction in scope-related change requests and ambiguity-driven defects across supported engagements.
- Stakeholder workshops facilitated and documented on schedule, with clear, actionable outputs.
- Requirements traceability maintained across all assigned projects, with consistent change tracking.
- Positive feedback from the Senior Project Manager and delivery leads on the quality and timeliness of analysis.
- On-time contribution to project planning and prioritisation cycles for supported engagements.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Senior Project Manager within the Cybersecurity BU's PM Team and works closely with project managers, technical delivery specialists, and client stakeholders to align business needs with delivered solutions. As an Individual Contributor role, it focuses on hands-on analysis and delivery rather than people management.
