## 1. Role Purpose

The Senior Presales Consultant is the technical engine behind the Cybersecurity BU's sales motion, translating complex offensive security, threat intelligence, DevSecOps, blockchain security, and consulting capabilities into compelling, buildable solutions for enterprise clients. By owning the technical narrative across the deal lifecycle, this role accelerates pipeline conversion, increases deal confidence, and ensures proposed architectures are both credible and deliverable. It is pivotal to scaling revenue as the BU progresses through its growth waves.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Lead technical engagement throughout the presales cycle, serving as the primary subject-matter expert who scopes client requirements and shapes winning cybersecurity and blockchain solutions.
- Design and document enterprise-grade technical proposals and solution architectures that map BU capabilities to client business outcomes and security objectives.
- Plan, build, and deliver compelling product demonstrations and proofs-of-concept (POCs) that validate proposed solutions and de-risk the client's buying decision.
- Partner closely with the Sales team to qualify opportunities, translate technical requirements into commercial proposals, and develop responses to RFPs, RFIs, and tenders.
- Build and maintain a reusable library of presales collateral, including solution blueprints, demo scripts, technical datasheets, and reference architectures.
- Articulate technical value to both engineering and executive audiences, leading whiteboard sessions, technical workshops, and capability briefings.
- Collaborate with delivery and consulting teams to ensure proposed scopes are accurate, feasible, and aligned with downstream delivery capacity.
- Track competitor offerings and market positioning to sharpen technical differentiation and strengthen the BU's win themes.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field.
- 5+ years of experience in presales, solution engineering, or a comparable technical sales-support function.
- Technical background in cybersecurity or blockchain.
- Strong presentation skills, with the ability to communicate complex concepts clearly to varied audiences.
- Demonstrated experience designing enterprise solutions.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Master's degree in a relevant field.
- Experience preparing and coordinating RFP/RFI responses.
- Working knowledge of the competitive cybersecurity and blockchain landscape.
- Hands-on experience developing demos and proofs-of-concept (POCs).

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Solution architecture and technical proposal development for enterprise environments.
- Familiarity with cybersecurity domains such as offensive security, threat intelligence, and DevSecOps, and/or blockchain security.
- Demo environment setup and POC scoping, build, and delivery.
- Requirements gathering, gap analysis, and translation of business needs into technical designs.
- Proficiency with proposal, diagramming, and presentation tooling for producing client-facing collateral.
- Understanding of secure architecture principles and relevant industry frameworks and standards.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- Clear, persuasive communication tailored to technical and executive stakeholders alike.
- Strong ownership and accountability for technical outcomes across the deal lifecycle.
- Collaborative mindset, working seamlessly across sales, delivery, and consulting teams.
- Analytical and structured thinking to break down ambiguous requirements into actionable solutions.
- Client-focused, consultative approach that builds trust and credibility.
- Adaptability and composure when managing competing priorities and tight tender deadlines.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**

- None mandatory.

**Preferred**

- Recognised cybersecurity certifications (e.g., CISSP, OSCP, or equivalent).
- Blockchain or cloud security credentials relevant to the BU's offerings.
- Vendor or solution-architecture certifications aligned to the proposed technology stack.

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Technical win rate on supported opportunities within the first 6–12 months.
- Quality and turnaround time of technical proposals, RFP/RFI responses, and solution architectures.
- Number and conversion rate of demos and POCs delivered to qualified prospects.
- Contribution to pipeline value and signed SOWs supported through presales engagement.
- Volume and reuse of presales collateral developed for the BU.
- Stakeholder satisfaction from sales and delivery teams on the quality of technical support provided.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports on a dotted line to the BU Head and works closely with the Sales team, alongside delivery, consulting, and technical specialists across the Cybersecurity BU. As a senior individual contributor, the consultant also acts as a technical mentor and reference point for less-experienced sales and presales colleagues.
