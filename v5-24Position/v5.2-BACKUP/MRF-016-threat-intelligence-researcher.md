## 1. Role Purpose

The Threat Intelligence Researcher exists to give the Cybersecurity BU and its clients an evidence-based understanding of the adversaries, malware, and techniques that threaten them. By conducting deep technical research and producing actionable intelligence products, this role sharpens the BU's detection, response, and advisory services and strengthens its reputation as a regional authority in cyber threat intelligence.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Conduct deep-dive research on threat actors, mapping their motivations, infrastructure, tooling, and campaign activity across regional and global landscapes.
- Analyse malware samples and attack techniques through static and dynamic analysis and reverse engineering to extract indicators, behaviours, and detection opportunities.
- Develop threat profiles, technical reports, and finished intelligence products that translate complex research into clear, actionable guidance for technical and non-technical audiences.
- Support incident response engagements by providing timely, context-rich intelligence on adversaries, tactics, and indicators of compromise.
- Track emerging threats, vulnerabilities, and attack methodologies, and proactively communicate relevant developments to internal teams and clients.
- Map observed adversary behaviour to frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK to enable consistent analysis and improved detection coverage.
- Contribute to the BU's thought leadership through published research, blogs, advisories, and conference or community engagement.
- Collaborate with offensive security, DevSecOps, and consulting teams to ensure intelligence findings are operationalised across the BU's services.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Cybersecurity, Computer Science, or a related field, or equivalent practical experience.
- 3+ years of experience in security research or threat intelligence.
- Hands-on experience with malware analysis and reverse engineering.
- Strong analytical and research skills, with the ability to draw sound conclusions from incomplete or ambiguous data.
- A track record of published research is an advantage.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Master's degree in a relevant field.
- Experience with threat actor profiling and attribution.
- Strong working knowledge of attack methodologies and the cyber kill chain.
- Conference speaking or public presentation experience.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Malware analysis and reverse engineering using tools such as IDA Pro, Ghidra, x64dbg, and OllyDbg.
- Static and dynamic analysis in sandboxed and isolated environments (e.g., Cuckoo, ANY.RUN, virtualised labs).
- Threat intelligence frameworks and methodologies, including MITRE ATT&CK, the Diamond Model, and the Cyber Kill Chain.
- Familiarity with threat intelligence platforms (e.g., MISP, OpenCTI) and IOC management.
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT) collection and analysis techniques.
- Scripting and automation for research workflows (e.g., Python).
- Understanding of network protocols, operating system internals, and common exploitation techniques.
- Clear technical writing for intelligence reporting and threat profiling.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- Analytical thinking — dissects complex problems and reasons rigorously from evidence to conclusion.
- Intellectual curiosity — driven to understand how adversaries operate and how threats evolve.
- Ownership — takes research from question to finished, decision-ready product with minimal supervision.
- Clear communication — conveys technical findings precisely to both expert and non-expert audiences.
- Collaboration — works effectively across CTI, offensive security, and consulting teams.
- Integrity — handles sensitive intelligence responsibly and represents findings honestly.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**
- None mandatory.

**Preferred**
- GIAC Reverse Engineering Malware (GREM)
- GIAC Cyber Threat Intelligence (GCTI)
- Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP) or equivalent technical certification

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Number and quality of intelligence products (threat profiles, technical reports, advisories) delivered within agreed timelines.
- Depth and accuracy of threat actor and malware research, validated through peer and lead review.
- Timeliness and usefulness of intelligence support provided to incident response engagements.
- Contribution to published research or thought leadership outputs during the first 6–12 months.
- Coverage of relevant emerging threats tracked and communicated to internal stakeholders and clients.
- Positive feedback from internal teams and clients on the relevance and actionability of intelligence delivered.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) Lead and works as an individual contributor within the CTI team, collaborating closely with offensive security, DevSecOps, incident response, and consulting colleagues to operationalise intelligence across the Cybersecurity BU.
