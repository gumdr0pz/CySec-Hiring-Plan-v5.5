## 1. WHAT CHANGED AND WHY

v5.0 Wave 1 was **platform-first**: it staffed delivery and coordination but had **no role accountable for winning work**. That created a circular dependency with our own Wave 2 trigger ("RM4M+ signed SOWs") — we expected RM2M of Q1 revenue and RM5M cumulative by Q2, yet the sellers (Sales + Presales) were not due until Wave 2. v5.0 also spent a scarce early seat on a **dedicated Senior PM** (pure overhead at 5 heads).

v5.1 re-sequences Wave 1 to **sell-and-deliver** — the standard playbook for standing up a services BU: *first hires must win work and deliver it billably; non-billable overhead follows utilization.*

**This is a re-sequencing, not a budget increase.** Every role moved is in the **Senior band**, so:

| | v5.0 | v5.1 | Change |
|---|------|------|--------|
| Wave 1 headcount | 5 | 5 | — |
| Wave 1 annual cost | RM423,360 – RM677,376 | RM423,360 – RM677,376 | **RM0** |
| Wave totals (W1/W2/W3) | 5 / 7 / 9 | 5 / 7 / 9 | — |
| Total programme cost | RM1,560,384 – RM2,496,614 | RM1,560,384 – RM2,496,614 | **RM0** |
| Billable seats in Wave 1 | ~2.5 / 5 | ~4 / 5 | **+1.5** |
| Pipeline ownership in Wave 1 | None | Presales + Sales | **Fixed** |

### Moves

- **Pulled UP into Wave 1:** Senior Presales Consultant (MRF-012), Senior Sales Specialist – Enterprise (MRF-010)
- **Deferred DOWN to Wave 2:** Government Security PM (MRF-001), Senior Project Manager (MRF-004)

> **Re-pull trigger:** if a government engagement anchors before Wave 2, pull **Gov Sec PM (MRF-001)** back into Wave 1 immediately — it becomes anchored revenue, not overhead. Swap it for whichever Wave 1 seat is least progressed.

## 3. COMPENSATION — FLAT BAND RETAINED (with mitigations)

Per decision, Wave 1 keeps the **flat Senior band (RM4,900 – RM7,840/mo base)** and hires all 5. This roster *reduces* the execution risk of a flat band because it **defers the hardest-to-fill, most under-market roles** (OffSec Lead Researcher, Red Team, CTI Lead) to Wave 2 — the five Wave 1 seats (consulting, PS, DevSecOps, presales, sales) are the most fillable at this band.

Residual risk remains on **DevSecOps** (tightest market). Mitigations — none of which touch base salary or breach the band policy:

1. **Sweeten total comp from headroom, not base.** With RM503K–1.44M of headroom under the 25% cap, fund sign-on bonuses, certification sponsorship (OSCP/CISSP), and a utilisation/revenue bonus. The *offer* lands without moving the band.
2. **Sequence DevSecOps first.** Give the tightest-market role the longest runway; pre-line a contractor bridge as a fallback.
3. **Re-rate offer-rejection risk to High** for DevSecOps and run a backup-candidate pool — never single-thread the critical hire.
