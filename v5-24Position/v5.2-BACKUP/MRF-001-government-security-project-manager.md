## 1. Role Purpose

The Government Security Project Manager leads the delivery of security and compliance engagements for public-sector and government clients within the Cybersecurity BU. The role exists to ensure that government security projects meet Malaysian regulatory frameworks, are delivered on time and within scope, and translate complex security requirements into actionable, well-governed programmes. By owning stakeholder relationships and compliance outcomes, this role strengthens the BU's credibility and growth in the government and critical-infrastructure segment.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- **Lead** government security compliance initiatives end-to-end, ensuring alignment with Malaysian government security frameworks and applicable regulatory mandates.
- **Develop** and maintain security policies, standards, and operating procedures that govern client engagements and meet public-sector assurance requirements.
- **Manage** security risk assessments across projects, identifying gaps, prioritising remediation, and tracking mitigation to closure.
- **Coordinate** with regulatory bodies and government authorities, serving as a trusted point of contact for audits, certifications, and compliance reviews.
- **Plan and oversee** government security project delivery, managing scope, schedule, budget, and resources to agreed milestones and SOW commitments.
- **Engage** government and enterprise stakeholders, translating technical security findings into clear, decision-ready briefings for senior and non-technical audiences.
- **Govern** project documentation, reporting, and quality controls to ensure full traceability and audit readiness throughout the engagement lifecycle.
- **Support** cross-functional delivery teams (offensive security, DevSecOps, consulting) by aligning their work to project objectives and compliance obligations.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Cybersecurity, or a related field.
- Minimum 5 years of experience in government security or compliance.
- Active CISSP, CISM, or equivalent industry certification.
- Working knowledge of Malaysian government security frameworks and regulatory expectations.
- Strong stakeholder management skills, with a demonstrated ability to engage government and senior client stakeholders.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Master's degree in a relevant field (Cybersecurity, Information Security, or related discipline).
- PMP or PRINCE2 certification.
- Prior experience working within or directly with the government sector.
- Experience supporting critical infrastructure protection (CIP) initiatives.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Sound understanding of information security governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) practices.
- Familiarity with security frameworks and standards such as ISO/IEC 27001, NIST CSF, and Malaysian public-sector security guidelines.
- Proficiency in security risk assessment methodologies and remediation planning.
- Competence with project management tooling (e.g., MS Project, Jira, Confluence) and structured delivery methodologies (waterfall and agile).
- Ability to develop policies, procedures, and compliance documentation to audit-grade quality.
- Awareness of offensive security, DevSecOps, and threat intelligence concepts sufficient to coordinate specialist delivery teams.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- **Stakeholder communication:** articulates complex security and compliance matters clearly to technical and executive audiences.
- **Ownership and accountability:** drives projects to completion and takes responsibility for delivery outcomes.
- **Analytical thinking:** assesses risk, weighs trade-offs, and makes sound, evidence-based decisions.
- **Collaboration:** works effectively across cross-functional teams and external regulatory bodies.
- **Integrity and discretion:** handles sensitive government and security information with appropriate confidentiality and ethical judgement.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**
- CISSP, CISM, or equivalent.

**Preferred**
- PMP or PRINCE2.

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- On-time, on-budget delivery of assigned government security projects against agreed milestones within the first 6–12 months.
- Compliance objectives met or exceeded across engagements, with zero critical audit or regulatory findings unresolved past agreed timelines.
- Completion of scheduled security risk assessments with documented, tracked remediation plans.
- Positive stakeholder and client satisfaction feedback from government and senior client contacts.
- Timely, audit-ready project documentation and reporting maintained throughout the engagement lifecycle.
- Effective coordination with regulatory bodies, evidenced by successful reviews, certifications, or approvals.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Principal Cybersecurity Architect and works closely with cross-functional delivery teams across offensive security, DevSecOps, threat intelligence, and consulting, as well as external government stakeholders and regulatory bodies. As a senior role, the Project Manager provides delivery leadership and guidance to project team members throughout government engagements.
