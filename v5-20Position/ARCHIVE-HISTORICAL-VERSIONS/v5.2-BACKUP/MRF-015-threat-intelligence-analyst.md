## 1. Role Purpose

The Threat Intelligence Analyst strengthens the Cybersecurity BU's Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) capability by turning raw threat data into actionable insight for our clients and internal teams. Sitting within the CTI team, this role helps customers anticipate, detect, and respond to emerging threats by tracking adversary activity, producing timely intelligence, and supporting proactive defence. The role directly underpins the BU's threat intelligence and professional services offerings as recurring revenue scales.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Analyse threat intelligence data from open-source, commercial, and internal feeds to identify relevant threats, indicators of compromise, and adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs).
- Monitor the evolving threat landscape across relevant industries and the Southeast Asian region to surface emerging risks and provide early warning to stakeholders.
- Produce clear, well-structured intelligence reports, briefings, and advisories tailored to both technical and executive audiences.
- Support threat hunting activities by supplying analysts with timely indicators, context, and hypotheses derived from intelligence findings.
- Maintain and curate threat intelligence databases, ensuring indicators, profiles, and source data are accurate, enriched, and up to date.
- Map observed activity to recognised frameworks (e.g., MITRE ATT&CK, the Cyber Kill Chain, the Diamond Model) to standardise analysis and reporting.
- Collaborate with detection, incident response, and consulting teams to operationalise intelligence and improve client security outcomes.
- Track threat actor and APT group activity to assess intent, capability, and potential impact on client environments.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Cybersecurity, Intelligence, or a related field.
- 2+ years of experience in threat intelligence or security analysis.
- Working knowledge of threat intelligence frameworks (e.g., MITRE ATT&CK, Cyber Kill Chain, Diamond Model).
- Strong analytical skills with the ability to assess, correlate, and prioritise threat data.
- Strong written communication skills, with proven ability to produce clear intelligence reports.
- Hands-on experience with SIEM tools.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- CTI or GCTI certification.
- Experience working with threat intelligence platforms (TIPs).
- Knowledge of known APT groups, their TTPs, and campaigns.
- Foreign language skills relevant to threat actor monitoring.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Threat intelligence collection, enrichment, and analysis across open-source (OSINT), commercial, and internal sources.
- Practical use of SIEM platforms for correlation, monitoring, and indicator validation.
- Application of intelligence frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK, the Cyber Kill Chain, and the Diamond Model.
- Familiarity with threat intelligence platforms (TIPs) and indicator/IOC management.
- Understanding of common attacker tooling, malware families, and APT group behaviours.
- Structured analytical writing for technical and executive audiences.
- Awareness of standards such as STIX/TAXII for sharing threat data (advantageous).

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- Analytical thinking: dissects complex, ambiguous information to reach sound, evidence-based conclusions.
- Clear communication: conveys technical findings concisely to varied audiences in writing and verbally.
- Ownership: takes initiative and accountability for assigned intelligence outputs and deadlines.
- Collaboration: works effectively across detection, response, and consulting teams.
- Curiosity and diligence: stays current with the threat landscape and validates sources rigorously.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**
- None mandatory.

**Preferred**
- CTI (Certified Threat Intelligence) or GIAC Cyber Threat Intelligence (GCTI).

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Deliver scheduled intelligence reports and advisories on time and to quality standards within the first 6 months.
- Maintain accuracy and freshness of threat databases, with indicators consistently enriched and validated.
- Provide actionable intelligence inputs that meaningfully support threat hunting and detection engagements.
- Achieve consistently positive feedback from internal teams and clients on the relevance and clarity of intelligence products.
- Demonstrate measurable coverage of the relevant threat landscape and timely early warning of emerging threats.
- Reduce time-to-insight from raw data to actionable reporting over the first 6–12 months.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Cyber Threat Intelligence Lead and operates as an individual contributor within the CTI team. The analyst collaborates closely with detection, incident response, threat hunting, and consulting colleagues to ensure intelligence is operationalised and delivers value to clients.
