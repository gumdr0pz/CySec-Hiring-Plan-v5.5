## 1. Role Purpose

The Senior DevSecOps Engineer embeds security into the heart of our software delivery lifecycle, ensuring that speed and safety advance together across the Cybersecurity BU's client and internal engagements. By designing automated, security-first pipelines and hardening cloud environments, this role directly strengthens the BU's offensive security, DevSecOps, and consulting service offerings. It is a hands-on senior position that translates security best practice into repeatable, scalable engineering outcomes for our clients in Kuala Lumpur and beyond.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- **Design and build** end-to-end DevSecOps pipelines that bake security controls and policy gates into every stage of development, testing, and deployment.
- **Integrate** static, dynamic, and dependency-scanning security tooling (SAST, DAST, SCA, secrets detection) seamlessly into CI/CD workflows with minimal disruption to delivery velocity.
- **Implement and harden** secure cloud configurations across AWS, Azure, and GCP, applying least-privilege, encryption, and compliance baselines.
- **Develop** continuous security monitoring, alerting, and logging systems that provide early detection of misconfigurations, vulnerabilities, and anomalous activity.
- **Automate** infrastructure provisioning and security remediation through scripting and infrastructure-as-code to reduce manual effort and configuration drift.
- **Author and maintain** clear security procedures, runbooks, and pipeline documentation that enable engineering teams and clients to operate securely and consistently.
- **Collaborate** with developers, architects, and client stakeholders to embed a shared-responsibility security culture and shift security left.
- **Advise and mentor** junior engineers, sharing DevSecOps expertise and raising the security maturity of project teams across the BU.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field.
- 5+ years of experience in DevOps, DevSecOps, or infrastructure security.
- Hands-on experience with CI/CD tooling such as Jenkins, GitLab CI, or GitHub Actions.
- Working knowledge of major cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP).
- Strong scripting skills in Python, Bash, and/or PowerShell.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Master's degree in a relevant field.
- Experience with containerization technologies (Docker, Kubernetes).
- Knowledge of infrastructure-as-code practices and tooling.
- Security certifications such as CISSP or CEH.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- CI/CD pipeline design and automation (Jenkins, GitLab CI, GitHub Actions).
- Security tooling integration: SAST, DAST, software composition analysis (SCA), and secrets scanning.
- Cloud security configuration and hardening across AWS, Azure, and GCP.
- Scripting and automation with Python, Bash, and PowerShell.
- Containerization and orchestration (Docker, Kubernetes) — advantageous.
- Infrastructure-as-code tooling (e.g., Terraform, CloudFormation, Ansible).
- Security monitoring, logging, and alerting (SIEM, cloud-native monitoring services).
- Familiarity with security frameworks and standards (OWASP, CIS Benchmarks, NIST).

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- **Ownership & accountability** — takes end-to-end responsibility for pipeline security outcomes and follows through to resolution.
- **Analytical thinking** — diagnoses root causes of vulnerabilities and configuration issues, balancing risk against delivery needs.
- **Collaboration** — partners effectively with developers, architects, and clients to embed security without becoming a bottleneck.
- **Clear communication** — explains security concepts and trade-offs to both technical and non-technical audiences.
- **Continuous improvement** — proactively stays current with evolving threats, tools, and DevSecOps practices.
- **Mentorship** — uplifts junior team members and champions a security-first engineering culture.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**
- None mandatory.

**Preferred**
- CISSP (Certified Information Systems Security Professional)
- CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker)

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Security tooling (SAST/DAST/SCA/secrets scanning) integrated into all priority CI/CD pipelines within the first 6 months.
- Measurable reduction in critical and high-severity vulnerabilities reaching production environments.
- Cloud security baselines and hardening standards documented and applied across active client and internal environments.
- Security monitoring and alerting coverage established for in-scope pipelines and infrastructure, with reduced mean time to detect.
- Reusable security procedures, runbooks, and pipeline templates published and adopted by delivery teams.
- Positive feedback from project teams and clients on security enablement and delivery velocity.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Principal Cybersecurity Architect and works closely with the DevSecOps team, software developers, cloud engineers, and client-facing consultants across the Cybersecurity BU. As a senior contributor, the Engineer also mentors junior team members and acts as a technical reference point for secure engineering practices.
