## 1. Role Purpose

The Red Team Specialist exists to emulate real-world adversaries against our clients' environments, exposing gaps that conventional testing misses. By designing and executing realistic attack campaigns, this role strengthens the Cybersecurity BU's offensive security capability and gives clients evidence-based confidence in their defences. The position directly supports the BU's growth in signed professional and consulting engagements while elevating the technical credibility of the OffSec team.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Plan and execute full-scope red team engagements and adversary simulations that replicate the tactics, techniques, and procedures of relevant threat actors.
- Develop tailored attack scenarios, playbooks, and tooling that align engagements to client risk profiles and the MITRE ATT&CK framework.
- Test the effectiveness of client detection, prevention, and response controls across networks, endpoints, applications, and cloud environments.
- Document findings, attack paths, and root causes in clear, audience-appropriate reports, and present actionable remediation recommendations to technical and executive stakeholders.
- Collaborate closely with blue teams during purple-team exercises to validate detections and drive measurable improvements in defensive posture.
- Maintain and evolve a library of attack scenarios, scripts, and methodologies to keep engagements aligned with the current threat landscape.
- Operate engagements safely and ethically, observing rules of engagement, scope boundaries, and legal and regulatory requirements throughout.
- Contribute to internal knowledge sharing and uplift junior team members through methodology reviews and hands-on guidance.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Cybersecurity, or a related field (equivalent practical experience considered).
- 5+ years of hands-on experience in red team operations.
- Demonstrated experience applying advanced attack techniques mapped to the MITRE ATT&CK framework.
- Strong technical documentation and reporting skills, with the ability to communicate findings to both engineers and decision-makers.
- Hold OSCP, OSCE, OSEP, or an equivalent offensive security certification.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Experience designing and delivering social engineering assessments.
- Knowledge of physical security testing methods and controls.
- Hands-on cloud security testing experience across major providers.
- Malware development or custom offensive tooling skills.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Adversary emulation and full attack lifecycle execution (initial access, persistence, privilege escalation, lateral movement, exfiltration).
- Practical command of the MITRE ATT&CK framework for scenario design and reporting.
- Offensive tooling such as Cobalt Strike, Metasploit, BloodHound, Sliver, and custom C2 frameworks.
- Network, Active Directory, endpoint, and application exploitation techniques.
- Scripting and tooling development in languages such as Python, PowerShell, C#, or Go.
- Cloud security testing across AWS, Azure, or GCP environments.
- Evasion, OPSEC, and defence-bypass techniques, with awareness of EDR/SIEM detection logic.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- Ownership and accountability for engagement outcomes, operating safely within agreed scope and rules of engagement.
- Analytical and creative problem-solving to identify non-obvious attack paths.
- Clear communication that translates complex technical findings for diverse audiences.
- Collaboration and a constructive purple-team mindset when working with defenders.
- Integrity and sound ethical judgement when handling sensitive client systems and data.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**
- OSCP, OSCE, OSEP, or an equivalent offensive security certification.

**Preferred**
- Advanced offensive certifications such as OSEE, CRTO, or GIAC GXPN / GPEN.
- Cloud security certifications relevant to red team operations.

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Deliver scoped red team engagements on time, within rules of engagement, and to client satisfaction within the first 6-12 months.
- Maintain high-quality, actionable reporting as measured by client and Lead Security Researcher feedback.
- Successfully demonstrate validated attack paths and control gaps in a majority of engagements.
- Contribute reusable attack scenarios, playbooks, or tooling to the OffSec library each quarter.
- Drive measurable detection or defensive improvements through purple-team collaboration with blue teams.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Lead Security Researcher and operates within the OffSec team, collaborating closely with blue teams, threat intelligence, and consulting colleagues across the Cybersecurity BU. As a senior contributor, the Red Team Specialist also shares methodologies and guides junior team members.
