## 1. Role Purpose

The Technical Account Manager (TAM) is the post-sale technical relationship owner for the Cybersecurity BU's clients. The TAM ensures clients realise measurable value from delivered cybersecurity and blockchain services, coordinates delivery across the technical teams, and protects and grows recurring revenue through high retention and expansion. As an early Wave 1 hire, the TAM operationalises the account-management layer that underpins the BU's RM8M+ recurring-revenue trigger for later waves.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Own the post-sale client relationship, acting as the primary point of contact for assigned accounts across the engagement lifecycle.
- Coordinate service delivery between clients and the BU's PM, Professional Services, and technical teams, ensuring commitments are met on time and to scope.
- Track account health — adoption, satisfaction, open issues, and renewal risk — and drive proactive interventions.
- Run regular check-ins and quarterly business reviews (QBRs), translating technical outcomes into client business value.
- Manage escalations end-to-end, marshalling the right technical resources and communicating clearly to stakeholders.
- Identify and qualify expansion and upsell opportunities, partnering with Sales and Presales to grow account value.
- Maintain account plans, success criteria, and renewal forecasts for assigned clients.
- Capture client feedback and feed it back into delivery and product improvement.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Information Technology, Business, or a related field (or equivalent experience).
- 2–4 years in technical account management, customer success, professional services, or technical support — ideally in cybersecurity, technology, or a B2B services environment.
- Working technical familiarity with cybersecurity and/or blockchain concepts sufficient to hold credible client conversations.
- Strong client-facing communication, coordination, and organisational skills.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Experience owning renewals and identifying expansion opportunities.
- Exposure to project coordination or delivery management.
- Familiarity with CRM and customer-success tooling.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Ability to interpret technical delivery work (assessments, DevSecOps, GRC, blockchain) and explain it in business terms.
- Account planning, health tracking, and renewal forecasting.
- Stakeholder management across technical and executive audiences.
- Escalation handling and cross-team coordination.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- Client-focused, consultative approach that builds trust.
- Proactive ownership and follow-through.
- Clear, calm communication under pressure.
- Collaborative across delivery, sales, and presales.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**

- None mandatory.

**Preferred**

- Customer-success or account-management certifications.
- Foundational cybersecurity or cloud credentials (e.g., Security+, relevant vendor certifications).

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Gross and net revenue retention across assigned accounts.
- Client satisfaction / NPS for managed accounts.
- On-time delivery and escalation resolution time.
- Expansion / upsell pipeline sourced from existing accounts.
- Renewal forecast accuracy.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role sits within Professional Services and reports on a dotted line to the BU Head. As the sole Professional Services hire, the TAM works closely with the PM team, Sales/Presales, and the technical delivery teams to coordinate service delivery and protect recurring revenue.

## 11. Career Progression

The natural next step is a Senior Technical Account Manager or Customer Success Lead role, with scope to grow into account-management or delivery leadership for the BU.
