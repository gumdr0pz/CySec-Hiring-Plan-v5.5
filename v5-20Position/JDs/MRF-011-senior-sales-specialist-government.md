## 1. Role Purpose

The Senior Sales Specialist (Government) drives revenue growth across Malaysia's public sector by positioning the Cybersecurity BU's offensive security, threat intelligence, DevSecOps, blockchain security, and consulting services within government agencies, ministries, and statutory bodies. The role exists to build trusted, long-term relationships with public sector stakeholders, navigate Malaysian government procurement, and convert tender opportunities into signed engagements. By owning the government sales pipeline, this role ensures the BU's services reach the agencies responsible for safeguarding national digital infrastructure.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Develop and nurture strategic relationships with government agencies, ministries, statutory bodies, and public sector decision-makers to establish the BU as a trusted cybersecurity partner.
- Identify, qualify, and prioritise government procurement and tender opportunities across the Malaysian public sector, maintaining a healthy and well-segmented pipeline.
- Lead the development of compelling, compliant proposals and bid responses for government tenders, ensuring alignment with agency requirements and procurement criteria.
- Achieve and exceed assigned government sales targets and revenue objectives, accurately forecasting and reporting pipeline progress to the BU Head.
- Navigate Malaysian government procurement frameworks, panels, and approval processes to position the BU's solutions effectively and shorten the path to award.
- Collaborate closely with the Government Security Project Manager to ensure proposed solutions are deliverable, scoped accurately, and transitioned smoothly from sale to delivery.
- Translate technical cybersecurity offerings into clear public sector value propositions, tailoring messaging to agency mandates and compliance obligations.
- Represent the BU at government engagements, industry briefings, and procurement events to build visibility and capture early opportunity signals.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Business, Public Administration, or a related field.
- Minimum 5 years of experience in government sector sales.
- Working knowledge of Malaysian government procurement processes and frameworks.
- Demonstrated experience selling security or technology solutions.
- Strong relationship-building skills with public sector stakeholders.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Master's degree in a relevant field.
- Previous experience working within or directly alongside government agencies.
- Knowledge of security compliance requirements relevant to the public sector.
- Hands-on tender and bid management experience.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Familiarity with cybersecurity service offerings, including offensive security, threat intelligence, DevSecOps, and consulting services.
- Proficiency in Malaysian government tendering, e-procurement portals, and panel/contract mechanisms.
- Proposal and bid writing, including pricing, scoping, and compliance matrices.
- Pipeline management and forecasting using CRM tools.
- Stakeholder mapping and account planning for complex, multi-decision-maker public sector accounts.
- Sound commercial acumen and contract negotiation skills.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- **Relationship Building:** Cultivates trust and long-term partnerships with senior public sector stakeholders.
- **Ownership & Accountability:** Takes full responsibility for the government pipeline and targets, driving deals to closure.
- **Communication & Influence:** Articulates complex security value clearly and persuasively to non-technical decision-makers.
- **Analytical Thinking:** Reads procurement landscapes and qualifies opportunities with rigour and judgement.
- **Collaboration:** Partners effectively with delivery and project management teams to ensure win-to-delivery continuity.
- **Resilience:** Maintains momentum through long government sales cycles and competitive tender processes.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**
- None mandatory.

**Preferred**
- Recognised sales methodology certification (e.g., Miller Heiman, SPIN, Sandler).
- Cybersecurity fundamentals certification (e.g., CompTIA Security+, ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity) to support solution conversations.

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Achievement of assigned government sales targets and signed SOW value within the first 6–12 months.
- Number of qualified government opportunities added to the pipeline per quarter.
- Tender submission volume and win rate against submitted bids.
- Breadth and depth of active government agency relationships established.
- Forecast accuracy against actual closed revenue.
- Smooth sale-to-delivery handover, measured by collaboration with the Government Security Project Manager.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports on a dotted line to the BU Head and sits within the Cybersecurity BU's Sales team. It works closely with the Government Security Project Manager and the wider delivery and technical teams to scope, propose, and transition government engagements.

The natural next step is progression to a Sales Lead or Government Sales Manager role, taking on a broader public sector portfolio and mentoring junior sales specialists within the Cybersecurity BU.
