## 1. Role Purpose

The Distributed Intelligence Analyst strengthens the Cybersecurity BU's Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) capability by collecting, analysing, and correlating intelligence from a wide range of distributed sources. The role exists to give the BU and its clients early, actionable visibility into emerging threats, threat actors, and indicators of compromise. By turning fragmented signals into coherent intelligence, the analyst directly supports the BU's recurring threat intelligence services and helps clients stay ahead of adversaries.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- **Collect** intelligence from distributed and open sources, applying OSINT and HUMINT methodologies to gather relevant, reliable, and timely information.
- **Monitor** dark web marketplaces, underground forums, and closed communities to detect threats, leaked data, and adversary activity targeting clients and the BU.
- **Track** threat indicators and indicators of compromise (IOCs), ensuring they are captured, validated, enriched, and made available to downstream detection and response functions.
- **Support** intelligence fusion and correlation by linking data across multiple sources to surface patterns, attribution insights, and emerging threat narratives.
- **Maintain** and tune intelligence collection capabilities, tooling, and source coverage to keep the BU's collection posture effective and current.
- **Produce** clear, well-structured intelligence notes and briefings that translate raw findings into actionable insights for analysts, clients, and stakeholders.
- **Collaborate** with CTI team members and adjacent security teams to validate findings and ensure intelligence is operationalised consistently.
- **Uphold** rigorous source handling, data integrity, and ethical/legal standards across all collection and analysis activities.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Cybersecurity, Intelligence Studies, or a related field.
- Minimum 2 years of experience in intelligence collection or analysis.
- Working knowledge of OSINT and HUMINT collection and analysis methodologies.
- Hands-on experience using intelligence tools and platforms for collection, enrichment, and correlation.
- Strong attention to detail and a disciplined, methodical approach to handling information.

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Experience with dark web and underground forum monitoring.
- Knowledge of threat indicator and IOC management practices.
- Proficiency in one or more foreign languages relevant to threat actor communities.
- Previous experience in an intelligence agency or comparable intelligence environment.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- OSINT and HUMINT collection techniques and tradecraft.
- Dark web and underground forum monitoring tools and safe access practices.
- Threat intelligence platforms (TIPs) and indicator/IOC management workflows.
- Familiarity with intelligence frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK, the Diamond Model, and the Cyber Kill Chain.
- Data correlation, enrichment, and intelligence fusion techniques.
- Structured analytic techniques and clear intelligence reporting.
- Awareness of common threat actor tactics, infrastructure, and IOC types (domains, hashes, IPs, indicators).

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- **Analytical thinking** — connects disparate signals into coherent, defensible assessments.
- **Attention to detail** — handles source data accurately and consistently under volume.
- **Curiosity and persistence** — pursues leads thoroughly across difficult and obscure sources.
- **Communication** — conveys findings clearly to both technical and non-technical audiences.
- **Integrity and discretion** — handles sensitive intelligence ethically and confidentially.
- **Collaboration** — works effectively within the CTI team and with adjacent security functions.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**
- None mandatory.

**Preferred**
- GIAC Open Source Intelligence (GOSI) or an equivalent OSINT certification.
- GIAC Cyber Threat Intelligence (GCTI).
- Other recognised threat intelligence or analyst certifications.

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Consistently meets agreed timeliness and quality standards for intelligence collection and reporting within the first 6 months.
- Maintains accurate, validated, and enriched IOC/threat indicator records with low false-positive rates.
- Delivers a steady cadence of actionable intelligence notes and briefings adopted by analysts and clients.
- Expands and sustains effective source coverage across distributed and dark web channels.
- Contributes measurable correlation and fusion insights that support detection, response, or client engagements.
- Demonstrates reliable, ethical, and compliant source handling with no integrity incidents.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Cyber Threat Intelligence Lead and works as an individual contributor within the CTI team, collaborating closely with fellow intelligence analysts and adjacent security functions across the Cybersecurity BU to validate findings and operationalise intelligence.
