## 1. Role Purpose

The Cyber Threat Intelligence Lead anchors the Cybersecurity BU's CTI capability, transforming raw indicators and global threat signals into actionable intelligence that protects clients and informs offensive, DevSecOps, and consulting engagements. This role exists to build a mature, repeatable intelligence function that anticipates adversary behaviour, sharpens detection and response, and positions the BU as a trusted intelligence partner across the Malaysian and regional market.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- **Establish and manage** the BU's end-to-end threat intelligence operations, defining collection priorities, sources, and tradecraft that align to client and business needs.
- **Develop and govern** structured threat intelligence frameworks (e.g. the intelligence cycle, MITRE ATT&CK, Diamond Model, F3EAD) to ensure consistent, defensible analytical outputs.
- **Lead** proactive threat hunting and analysis activities, correlating internal telemetry with external intelligence to surface emerging adversary tactics, techniques, and campaigns.
- **Produce** clear, decision-ready intelligence reports, briefings, and advisories tailored to technical teams, executives, and external clients.
- **Operate and optimise** the threat intelligence platform (MISP, OpenCTI), ensuring high-quality enrichment, deduplication, and timely dissemination of indicators across the organisation.
- **Track and assess** geopolitical and sector-specific threats relevant to Malaysian and regional clients, translating context into prioritised risk guidance.
- **Collaborate** with offensive security, DevSecOps, blockchain security, and consulting teams to embed intelligence-driven insight into engagements, detections, and client deliverables.
- **Mentor and develop** analysts within the CTI team, raising analytical rigour, writing standards, and operational maturity across the function.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Cybersecurity, Intelligence, or a related field.
- 7+ years of professional experience in threat intelligence.
- Demonstrated hands-on experience with threat intelligence platforms such as MISP and OpenCTI.
- Strong analytical reasoning and excellent written communication skills, with a track record of producing intelligence reports for varied audiences.
- Holds CTI, GCTI, or an equivalent threat intelligence certification (see Section 7).

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Master's degree in a relevant field (Cybersecurity, Intelligence Studies, International Relations, or similar).
- Prior experience working with or alongside law enforcement or intelligence agencies.
- Working knowledge of geopolitical threats and their cyber implications.
- Proficiency in one or more foreign languages to support open-source collection and analysis.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Threat intelligence platforms: MISP, OpenCTI (administration, tuning, and integration).
- Intelligence frameworks and models: MITRE ATT&CK, Diamond Model, Cyber Kill Chain, F3EAD, and the intelligence cycle.
- Threat hunting and analysis across endpoint, network, and log telemetry.
- Indicator lifecycle management: collection, enrichment, normalisation, and dissemination (STIX/TAXII).
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT) tradecraft and dark/deep web research.
- Familiarity with SIEM, EDR, and detection engineering workflows.
- Strong reporting and visualisation skills for both technical and executive consumption.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- **Analytical thinking** — synthesises ambiguous, high-volume data into clear, confidence-rated assessments.
- **Communication** — conveys complex intelligence concisely to technical and non-technical stakeholders.
- **Ownership** — drives the CTI function with accountability for quality, timeliness, and operational reliability.
- **Leadership & mentorship** — develops junior analysts and sets the standard for analytical tradecraft.
- **Collaboration** — partners effectively across offensive security, DevSecOps, and consulting teams.
- **Integrity & discretion** — handles sensitive intelligence and client data with sound judgement.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**

- CTI, GCTI, or an equivalent threat intelligence certification.

**Preferred**

- GIAC Cyber Threat Intelligence (GCTI), GIAC Open Source Intelligence (GOSI), or comparable advanced credentials.
- Relevant analyst or intelligence-specific certifications complementing the CTI domain.

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Stand up a documented, framework-aligned threat intelligence operation within the first 6 months.
- Achieve full operational use of the threat intelligence platform (MISP/OpenCTI), with measurable improvement in indicator quality and enrichment coverage.
- Deliver a regular cadence of intelligence reports and advisories meeting agreed quality and turnaround standards.
- Demonstrate intelligence-driven contributions to client engagements and internal detection improvements.
- Establish and run a repeatable threat hunting programme with documented findings within 12 months.
- Uplift CTI team capability through mentoring, evidenced by analyst output and skills progression.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Principal Cybersecurity Architect and leads the CTI team, working closely with the offensive security, DevSecOps, blockchain security, and professional services functions. As a Lead, the role mentors and develops CTI analysts and sets analytical and operational standards for the intelligence function.
