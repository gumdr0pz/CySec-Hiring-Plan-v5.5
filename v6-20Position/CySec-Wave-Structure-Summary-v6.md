## Design Principle: Supportive at Every Stage

Each wave stands up functional capability that can deliver, with the org-chart core landing first:

- **Wave 1 (8):** The near-term core drawn on the CTO org chart — Solutions Architect + Presales Engineer, Staff Engineer DevSecOps + Engineer DevSecOps, GRC Specialist, Consultant – Government Security, Product Manager, and a Technical Account Manager for post-sale delivery.
- **Wave 2 (5):** Revenue closers (Account Manager – Enterprise + Government) and senior cyber-core practice leadership (Offensive Security Team Lead + Staff Engineer, Threat Intelligence Team Lead) — triggered by signed SOWs.
- **Wave 3 (7):** The scale-up bench — engineering/IC across Offensive Security, Threat Intelligence, GRC, and PM, plus the Blockchain Staff Engineer.

> **General vs cyber-core:** Support functions (Solutions/Presales, DevSecOps, Blockchain, Product/PM, Sales, Professional Services) are **general roles with a dotted line** to the practice; sensitive work is compartmentalised, a future spinoff handled by secondment. **Cyber-core** (Offensive Security, Threat Intelligence, GRC, Government Security) sits **inside** the practice. Titles follow the company convention; levels follow the HR framework; salary is the Rate-card benchmark. The former Junior PM is replaced by a **Product Manager** (Manager level).
