## 1. Direction (decisions taken)

1. **General roles, not cyber-dedicated.** The org isn't large enough for dedicated cyber versions of cross-functional roles. Support roles (DevSecOps, Presales/Solutions, Product Manager, Business Analyst, Blockchain) are **general** with a **dotted line** to the Cybersecurity practice. Sensitive work is **compartmentalised**; a future spinoff is handled by **secondment**.
2. **Cyber-core stays in the practice:** Offensive Security, Threat Intelligence, GRC, Government Security.
3. **Titles follow the company convention** (Engineer / Senior Engineer / Staff Engineer / Specialist / Solutions Architect / Product Manager / Team Lead / Manager); the role scope reflects the actual work.
4. **PM = Product Manager** (Manager level), general with dotted line to cyber. Business Analyst retained; Junior PM removed.
5. **Salary = Rate-card "Rate" tab benchmark** (Assumptions tab is outdated). Pay at full benchmark; the **25% revenue cap is relaxed** to accommodate it (see §6).
6. **Phasing:** roles that appear on the CTO org chart (Solutions Architect, DevSecOps, GRC Specialist, Product Manager) are the **near-term core (Wave 1)**; the rest are growth (Waves 2–3).

## 2. HR Job-Level Framework

Job level is distinct from salary band. The **Specialist** rung sits **one level above Senior Executive** (Level 4) as a **dual Specialist / Team Lead** designation — Specialist on the IC track, Team Lead for the people-management track.

| Level | HR Band | Mgmt-track title | IC-track title | Monthly benchmark (RM) |
|------:|---------|------------------|----------------|------------------------|
| 7 | C-Suite | — | — | 55,000 – 72,000 |
| 6 | Senior Manager | Senior Manager | Principal Engineer | 22,000 – 28,000 |
| 5 | Manager | Manager | Senior Staff Engineer / Solutions Architect | 17,500 – 20,400 |
| **4** | **Assistant Manager** | **Team Lead** | **Specialist** / Staff Engineer | **11,500 – 15,500** |
| 3 | Senior Executive | — | Senior Engineer (Consultant if client-facing) | 8,000 – 11,000 |
| 2 | Executive (upper) | — | Engineer II | ~7,000 |
| 1 | Executive | — | Engineer | 4,000 – 7,000 |

*Levels 1–2 always carry an Engineer title; Levels 3+ are customer-facing and may carry a Consultant title. Benchmark = "Monthly Salary (Ref)" from the Rate tab (gross, monthly).*

## 6. Budget / Cap Reconciliation

| Measure | Value | vs RM12M revenue |
|---|---|---|
| Annual base salary | RM2.26M – RM3.07M | 18.9% – 25.6% |
| Annual burdened (×1.44) | **RM3.26M – RM4.43M** | **27.1% – 36.9%** |

At full benchmark, burdened cost **exceeds the old 25% cap (RM3.0M)** at every point. Per decision, the cap is **relaxed**. Two levers to frame it for the board:

- **Raise the cap** to ~**37%** of revenue (covers the max-benchmark scenario), or
- **Raise the revenue target** to ~**RM17.7M** (the level at which max burdened cost = 25%).

At the current RM12M target, plan for a labour ratio of **~27–37%** and treat the Rate-card figures as the market benchmark that actual offers sit within.
