## 1. Role Purpose

The Product Manager – Government Security owns the BU's government-security product and service offerings end to end — defining the roadmap, requirements, and priorities that turn complex public-sector security and compliance needs into deliverable, well-governed solutions. The role ensures offerings meet Malaysian regulatory frameworks, align to government client outcomes, and are sequenced for sustainable delivery. By owning the product direction, stakeholder relationships, and compliance outcomes, this role strengthens the BU's credibility and growth in the government and critical-infrastructure segment. *(Note: this is a Product Manager role; the BU is not hiring dedicated Project Managers at this stage.)*

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- **Own the product roadmap** for government-security offerings, prioritising features and engagements against client value, compliance obligations, and BU strategy.
- **Define and manage requirements**, translating government client needs and regulatory mandates into clear product specifications, user stories, and acceptance criteria.
- **Govern compliance outcomes**, ensuring offerings align with Malaysian government security frameworks and applicable regulatory mandates.
- **Coordinate with regulatory bodies and government authorities** as a trusted point of contact for audits, certifications, and compliance reviews.
- **Manage the backlog and release planning**, sequencing delivery with the cyber-core and general delivery teams to agreed outcomes and SOW commitments.
- **Engage government and enterprise stakeholders**, translating technical security findings into clear, decision-ready briefings for senior and non-technical audiences.
- **Maintain product documentation, reporting, and quality controls** to ensure full traceability and audit readiness across the offering lifecycle.
- **Partner with cross-functional teams** (offensive security, DevSecOps, threat intelligence, GRC, consulting) to align their work to the product roadmap and compliance objectives.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Cybersecurity, or a related field.
- Minimum 5 years of experience in government security, compliance, or security product/programme ownership.
- Active CISSP, CISM, or equivalent industry certification.
- Working knowledge of Malaysian government security frameworks and regulatory expectations.
- Strong stakeholder management skills, with a demonstrated ability to engage government and senior client stakeholders.

## 4. Preferred Qualifications

- Master's degree in a relevant field (Cybersecurity, Information Security, or related discipline).
- Product management certification (e.g., Pragmatic, SAFe POPM) or equivalent product-ownership experience.
- Prior experience working within or directly with the government sector.
- Experience supporting critical infrastructure protection (CIP) initiatives.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- Sound understanding of information security governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) practices.
- Familiarity with security frameworks and standards such as ISO/IEC 27001, NIST CSF, and Malaysian public-sector security guidelines.
- Product-management skills: roadmapping, requirements/backlog management, and prioritisation.
- Proficiency in security risk assessment methodologies and remediation planning.
- Competence with product/backlog tooling (e.g., Jira, Confluence) and agile delivery practices.
- Awareness of offensive security, DevSecOps, and threat intelligence concepts sufficient to direct specialist delivery teams.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- **Stakeholder communication:** articulates complex security and compliance matters clearly to technical and executive audiences.
- **Ownership and accountability:** drives the product roadmap to outcomes and takes responsibility for delivery and compliance results.
- **Analytical thinking:** assesses risk, weighs trade-offs, and makes sound, evidence-based prioritisation decisions.
- **Collaboration:** works effectively across cross-functional teams and external regulatory bodies.
- **Integrity and discretion:** handles sensitive government and security information with appropriate confidentiality and ethical judgement.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**
- CISSP, CISM, or equivalent.

**Preferred**
- Product management / product ownership certification.

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Government-security roadmap delivered against agreed priorities and milestones within the first 6–12 months.
- Compliance objectives met or exceeded across offerings, with zero critical audit or regulatory findings unresolved past agreed timelines.
- Requirements and backlog maintained with clear, testable acceptance criteria accepted by delivery teams.
- Positive stakeholder and client satisfaction feedback from government and senior client contacts.
- Timely, audit-ready product documentation and reporting maintained across the offering lifecycle.
- Effective coordination with regulatory bodies, evidenced by successful reviews, certifications, or approvals.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Head of Cybersecurity Practice and works closely with cross-functional teams across offensive security, DevSecOps, threat intelligence, GRC, and consulting, as well as external government stakeholders and regulatory bodies. As a Level 4 role, the Product Manager provides product leadership and direction to the teams delivering government-security offerings.
