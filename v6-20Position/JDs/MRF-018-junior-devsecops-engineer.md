## 1. Role Purpose

The Engineer – DevSecOps helps embed security into the heart of our software delivery and infrastructure pipelines, ensuring that protection is built in rather than bolted on. Working alongside the Staff Engineer – DevSecOps, this role supports the automation, monitoring, and documentation that keep the BU's DevSecOps practice reliable and audit-ready. It is an ideal entry point for an early-career engineer to grow technical depth across CI/CD, cloud, and security operations while delivering tangible value to client and internal platforms.

This is a **general role with a dotted-line responsibility to the Cybersecurity Practice**: it serves BU-wide delivery and supports cyber-sensitive engagements through compartmentalised work where required.

## 2. Key Responsibilities

- Support the design, build, and maintenance of secure CI/CD pipelines, integrating security controls and quality gates into the software delivery lifecycle.
- Develop and implement security automation scripts that streamline repetitive tasks such as scanning, configuration checks, and remediation workflows.
- Monitor security alerts and incidents across pipelines and cloud environments, triaging findings and escalating issues to senior engineers in a timely manner.
- Document security procedures, runbooks, and pipeline configurations to maintain clear, current, and audit-ready operational records.
- Participate actively in infrastructure reviews, contributing observations on security posture, misconfigurations, and opportunities for hardening.
- Assist in integrating security tooling (e.g., SAST, DAST, dependency and container scanning) into existing workflows under senior guidance.
- Collaborate with development and operations colleagues to resolve security findings and promote secure-by-default practices across teams.
- Build technical knowledge of cloud platforms, containerization, and infrastructure as code to strengthen contributions over time.

## 3. Required Qualifications & Experience

- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field.
- 2+ years of experience in DevOps or security.
- Basic working knowledge of CI/CD tools and pipeline concepts.
- Scripting skills in one or more of Python, Bash, or PowerShell.
- Foundational understanding of at least one major cloud platform (AWS, Azure, or GCP).

## 4. Preferred / Desirable Qualifications

- Security certifications such as CompTIA Security+ or CEH.
- Hands-on experience with containerization (e.g., Docker, Kubernetes).
- Knowledge of infrastructure as code (e.g., Terraform, CloudFormation, Ansible).
- Practical automation scripting experience in a production or lab environment.

## 5. Technical Skills & Competencies

- CI/CD tooling such as Jenkins, GitLab CI, GitHub Actions, or Azure DevOps.
- Scripting and automation with Python, Bash, and/or PowerShell.
- Cloud fundamentals across AWS, Azure, or GCP (compute, networking, IAM basics).
- Familiarity with security scanning approaches (SAST, DAST, dependency and container scanning).
- Exposure to containerization and orchestration concepts (Docker, Kubernetes).
- Basic understanding of infrastructure as code and version control with Git.
- Awareness of common security frameworks and the principle of secure-by-default design.

## 6. Behavioural Competencies

- Eagerness to learn, with a proactive approach to closing knowledge gaps and absorbing feedback.
- Clear written and verbal communication, particularly when documenting procedures and reporting findings.
- Ownership and reliability in following through on assigned tasks to completion.
- Collaborative mindset, working effectively with senior engineers, developers, and operations colleagues.
- Analytical thinking and attention to detail when triaging alerts and reviewing configurations.

## 7. Certifications

**Required**

- None mandatory.

**Preferred**

- CompTIA Security+
- Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)
- Foundational cloud certification (e.g., AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner, Azure Fundamentals)

## 8. Key Performance Indicators

- Contribute to and maintain assigned CI/CD pipeline components with security controls integrated.
- Deliver functional security automation scripts that reduce manual effort on recurring tasks.
- Maintain accurate, up-to-date documentation for procedures and pipelines covering owned areas.
- Meet agreed response and triage timelines for monitored security alerts and incidents.
- Demonstrate measurable growth in cloud, containerization, or IaC competency within the first 6–12 months.
- Participate consistently and usefully in infrastructure reviews.

## 9. Reporting & Team Context

This role reports to the Staff Engineer – DevSecOps (with a dotted line to the Head of Cybersecurity Practice) and works closely with the wider DevSecOps team as well as development and operations colleagues across the BU. As a Level 1 Engineer position, the focus is on learning and contributing under senior guidance rather than leading others.
